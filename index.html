Step 1: Data Collection

We loaded the StudentsPerformance.csv dataset, which includes details like gender, race, parental education, lunch type, and scores for math, reading, and writing.

Step 2: Creating a Total Score Column

We added a new column total_score by summing the three subject scores. This helped us understand the overall performance of each student.

Step 3: Plotting Total Score Distribution

We used a histogram to visualize how total scores are distributed among students. It showed us if the scores were clustered or spread out.

Step 4: Comparing Genders

We calculated and compared average scores by gender for each subject to see performance differences between male and female students.

Step 5: Correlation Analysis

A correlation heatmap helped us identify relationships between numerical fields—showing which scores tend to increase together.

Key Insights

  • Math scores have the highest impact on a student's total score.
  • Girls perform better in reading and writing compared to boys.
  • Parental education level and gender show moderate effects on student performance.
